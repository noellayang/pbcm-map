What the map documents

A growing record of practical change.

Lighting Up Canada brings together plant-based food policies, procurement decisions, menu transformations, and institutional commitments from across the country.

The map focuses on documented outcomes: what is happening, where it is happening, and what others can learn from it.

Municipalities

Motions, procurement policies, public events, and municipal food service initiatives.

Educational institutions

Universities, colleges, schools, and campus dining programs advancing plant-forward food.

Healthcare

Patient menus, hospital pilots, food-service practices, and health-centred institutional change.

Government and public bodies

Agencies, public authorities, and other bodies shaping food standards, programs, or procurement.

Hospitality and other institutions

Organizations, facilities, and public-facing programs showing additional pathways for progress.

Why visibility matters

Progress moves faster when institutions can see what is already possible.

Many initiatives begin in isolation. By bringing examples together, Lighting Up Canada helps institutions learn from one another, adapt proven ideas, and move more confidently from intention to action.

Built from public evidence

A resource designed for trust, learning, and responsible use.

Each published entry is supported by at least one publicly available source, such as a policy, council record, institutional report, announcement, or news article.

Read the methodology
  1. 01 Document a clear policy, program, menu change, procurement decision, or institutional commitment.
  2. 02 Link each public entry to at least one source that visitors can review directly.
  3. 03 Record the year or timeframe of implementation where the information is available.
  4. 04 Focus on the documented outcome while treating attribution as useful supporting context.
